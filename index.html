Introducing CredB

CredB is a bag that hold all your wallets, passports, and other collections of credentials. CredB may stand for "credentials bag" but credb.io (”Cred Bio”) also stands for "credible biography". CredB is also like a keychain, as it is where the user controls all the keys that control their DIDs. From an application perspective , CredB is a wallet of wallets; from a device perspective, CredB is a phone within a phone.

Frequently Asked Questions

Below, you'll find answers to some of the most common questions about CredB, a digital solution that enables individuals to securely manage their digital identities and important documents. Whether you're curious about CredB's security features or how it compares to other solutions, we've got you covered.

  • Can I trust CredB with my personal and sensitive information?

    You do not need to trust CredB with your personal or sensitive information, because CredB cannot see it. CredB does not store your information; you store it on DIDs that you curate and control via CredB, which merely as the machinery blindly following your instructions. You control your digital keys, which are literally the keys to your kingdom. CredB helps you manage these keys so your information is safe, forever. Meanwhile, CredB also makes sure that your information is useful to others, by providing assurance on the trustworthiness of your information, to maximize its utility value.

  • What happens if there is a security breach or data leak in CredB?

    CredB does not store any information, so there is no data to leak. CredB authentication is between the user and the device only (hence, please be very careful with your password to unlock CredB, though losing it isn't the end of the world). Your sensitive data is not stored with CredB, but rather on DIDs. In the Web2 world, a hacker can hack a database of a million users to get a million users' data; to get a million CredB users' data, a hacker needs to hack a million CredB users. How? Through compromised digital keys. But don't worry, CredB will help you prevent this from happening or mitigate the damage if it does happen.

  • Is CredB compatible with the digital services and platforms I use?

    CredB exceeds the requirements and capabilities of Web2 applications, so by default, most products and services would not be compatible, but this is rapidly changing as organizations race to convert Web2 applications to be Web3 ready or create new products and services in adherence to Web3 design patterns. The number of compatible digital services and platforms will only rise, and fast. If you are a developer, a CIO, or anyone in-between looking to spearhead your organization's evolution into the Web3 world, please say hello@credb.io and let's us know how we may be helpful!

  • Will my personal information be protected and kept private in CredB?

    CredB is not a centralised identity service like the Big Tech of the Web2 era. CredB does not store any personal information; it is not possible by design. CredB facilitates regular folks from all walks of life to manage their own personal information, and to facilitate its use in a privacy-preserving manner while providing assurance on the trustworthiness of your information, to maximize its utility value.

  • Does CredB comply with applicable data protection and privacy laws?

    CredB is built in adherence to the Principle of Privacy-By-Design and Privacy-By-Default. The team religiously follows the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). Additionally, CredB is built to be compliant with the most stringent regulations in the world: the financial industry. Thus, CredB could credibly claim to exceed the applicable data protection and privacy laws of many jurisdictions, or at least be compliant to those of yours.
